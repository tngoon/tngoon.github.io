Welcome to CritiqueKit!

In this task, you will be providing feedback for student submissions submitted to the Interaction Design Specialization on Coursera. CritiqueKit provides you with real-time categorization and feedback suggestions to help you give more helpful and complete feedback. This tutorial will show you how to make the most of your experience with CritiqueKit's features.


Some tips: Good feedback is...

  • Specific: E.g., instead of "looks great!"... try, "Great use of colors and imagery on the site."
  • Actionable: E.g., instead of "The site is too cramped."... try, "Maybe use a 2-column grid layout instead of a 3-column layout to better space out elements."
  • Justified: E.g., instead of "I wish the site was a different color."... try, "I wish the site was a different color because the current color is too dark and makes the dark text difficult to read."

Continue on to learn more about CritiqueKit's features.

Your goal is to try to make sure your feedback fits the three categories of: Specific, Actionable, and Justified for the most helpful and complete feedback. You can hover over each checkbox to learn more about what each characteristic means.


To help you get started, you can select the feedback suggestions under "Well Done" and "For Improvement", which will copy it directly into the feedback textbox.

As you provide your feedback, the checkboxes will automatically check if CritiqueKit detects that your comment contains any of the characteristics.


If your feedback doesn't fit a certain characteristic, CritiqueKit will provide you with feedback suggestions provided from others to help you complete your comment.


The Submit button also changes color to yellow to inform you that your comment can be further improved.

Once your feedback fits all the characteristics, your comment will be considered complete, and the Submit button will turn green.


Submit your comment and continue providing additional feedback comments or click the "I'm done" button at the top of the page to finish giving feedback. You can view all your submitted comments by clicking on the "View my comments" button.
